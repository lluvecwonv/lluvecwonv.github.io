# DE-COP: Detecting Copyrighted Content in Language Models Training Data

**Venue:** ICML 2024
**Authors:** André V. Duarte, Xuandong Zhao, Arlindo L. Oliveira, Lei Li
**Affiliations:** INESC-ID / Instituto Superior Técnico (ULisboa), University of California Santa Barbara, Carnegie Mellon University
**Code:** [GitHub](https://github.com/LeiLiLab/DE-COP)

![DE-COP Overview](/figures/decop/CrownImage_small_borders.png)

---

## One-Line Summary

A novel method called **DE-COP** that detects whether copyrighted content was used in LLM training by probing the model with **multiple-choice questions** (verbatim text vs. paraphrases), applicable to both open-source and **fully black-box** models.

---

## 1. Overview and Motivation

As LLMs are trained on increasingly massive datasets, ensuring that all collected data meets ethical and legal standards becomes progressively difficult. The incorporation of copyrighted content without crediting creators has led to real-world lawsuits, such as The New York Times vs. OpenAI and class actions against Stable Diffusion.

Existing detection approaches face key limitations:

**Token probability-based methods (e.g., Min-K%-Prob):** Based on the premise that the least probable tokens of training data examples have higher average log-likelihoods. Effective but **requires access to token probabilities**, making them inapplicable to fully black-box models like ChatGPT and Claude.

**Prompting-based methods:** Attempt to make models "reveal" memorized content, but models are continually updated with internal monitoring systems that flag and refuse such prompts. Additionally, extracting many examples from the same document to demonstrate clear copyright infringement is difficult.

DE-COP overcomes both limitations: it is **applicable to any LLM** while identifying a substantial amount of potentially copyrighted content.

---

## 2. Benchmarks: BookTection and arXivTection

### 2.1 BookTection

BookTection comprises passages from **165 books** with AI-generated paraphrases.

**Core principle:** Books published post-2023 are definitively **non-member data**, while those published before or during 2021 may potentially be **member data**. Books from 2022 are excluded due to ambiguity around some models' knowledge cutoff dates (e.g., LLaMA-2's cutoff is September 2022).

**Data composition:**
- Starting from 90 titles from the BookMIA benchmark
- 75 additional books: 15 recently published + 60 high-grossing bestsellers
- Average of **34 random passages** per book
- Preprocessing: HTML cleanup, punctuation enforcement, word length compliance

**Three length settings:**
- **Short:** ~64 tokens
- **Medium:** ~128 tokens
- **Long:** ~256 tokens

Each original passage comes with **3 paraphrases generated by Claude 2.0** and a ground truth label.

### 2.2 arXivTection

A proof-of-concept dataset of **50 arXiv papers** (25 from 2023, 25 pre-2022). Since arXiv papers are standard inclusions in training data (e.g., The Pile), this serves to validate DE-COP's reliability before applying it to the less certain book detection task. Passages are approximately 128 tokens in length.

---

## 3. DE-COP Methodology

### 3.1 Core Idea

DE-COP is influenced by counterfactual memorization studies. The key premise:

> If a model can correctly identify verbatim text from among paraphrased versions **far more frequently** when the text was in its training data compared to when it was not, this indicates the text was likely used in training.

### 3.2 Full Pipeline

![DE-COP Pipeline](/figures/decop/Pipeline2.png)

**Step 1 — Dataset Construction:**
1. Extract passages from various books
2. Generate **3 paraphrased versions** of each passage using Claude 2

**Step 2 — Model Evaluation:**
1. Construct **4-option MCQA** questions: original passage + 3 paraphrases
2. Generate **24 permutations** of all possible option orderings to address selection bias (models' tendency to prefer certain answer positions like "A")
3. Compute **baseline performance** on "clean" books known to not be in training data

**Step 3 — Detection:**
1. Apply the same procedure to suspect books
2. Compare suspect book performance against the baseline
3. **Significantly higher accuracy** → likely present in training data

### 3.3 Logit Calibration

![Logit Calibration](/figures/decop/LogitTuning.png)

While DE-COP is designed primarily for fully black-box settings, open-source models allow additional debiasing through logit calibration:

1. Select 30 unseen books (separate from baseline computation set)
2. Theoretically, the model should assign uniform probability across A, B, C, D labels for unseen data
3. Observe the empirical bias in label distribution and compute **prior adjustments**
4. Apply these adjustments to label probabilities before selecting the most probable answer on new examples

## 4. Experimental Setup

### 4.1 Statistical Evaluation

For each book, accuracy is computed from all model responses. For a book with 30 passages, each evaluated with 24 permutations, this yields **720 model responses**. Book-level accuracy is the proportion of correct predictions.

**Bootstrapping (10 iterations):** In each iteration, sample with replacement from both groups, determine an optimal threshold θ for separation, and compute AUC. Report mean and standard deviation of AUC across iterations, along with **t-test p-values** for group separation.

### 4.2 Models Evaluated

**Open-source (logit access):** Mistral 7B, Mixtral 8x7B, LLaMA-2 13B, LLaMA-2 70B, GPT-3

**Closed-source (fully black-box):** ChatGPT, Claude 2.1

### 4.3 Baselines

**For logit-accessible models:** Perplexity, Zlib ratio, Lowercase ratio, Min-K%-Prob

**For black-box models:** Prefix Probing (32/50-token prefix → generation → Token Sort similarity ≥80%), Modified Name Cloze task

### 4.4 Implementation Details

- Paraphrase generation: `temperature=0.1`
- Model evaluation: `temperature=0`
- 4× NVIDIA A100 80GB GPUs, no quantization needed

## 5. Results

### 5.1 Proof of Concept — arXivTection

| Measure | Claude 2.1 | LLaMA-2 70B | Mixtral 8x7B |
|---------|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| AUC | 0.908 (±0.038) | 0.726 (±0.041) | 0.736 (±0.089) |
| p-value | 3.13×10⁻²⁹ | 1.04×10⁻¹² | 5.50×10⁻⁰⁷ |

All three models effectively distinguish between training and non-training arXiv papers, with extremely low p-values allowing confident rejection of the null hypothesis. This validates DE-COP's reliability for the BookTection benchmark.

### 5.2 Main Results — Models with Logit Access (BookTection-128)

| Method | Mistral 7B | Mixtral 8x7B | LLaMA-2 13B | LLaMA-2 70B | GPT-3 | Avg. |
|--------|-----------|-------------|------------|------------|-------|------|
| Perplexity | 0.724 | 0.829 | 0.783 | 0.892 | 0.874 | 0.820 |
| Zlib | 0.599 | 0.690 | 0.630 | 0.747 | 0.779 | 0.689 |
| Lowercase | 0.846 | 0.889 | 0.880 | 0.927 | **0.957** | 0.900 |
| Min-K%-Prob | 0.763 | 0.844 | 0.798 | 0.895 | 0.898 | 0.840 |
| **DE-COP** | **0.901** | **0.968** | **0.900** | **0.972** | 0.863 | **0.921** |

**Key findings:**
- DE-COP achieves **best AUC on all models except GPT-3**
- Average AUC of **0.921**, a **9.6% improvement** over Min-K%-Prob (0.840)
- GPT-3 experiment ran on only 70 books due to API costs — likely an underestimate of DE-COP's performance

### 5.3 Main Results — Fully Black-Box Models (BookTection-128)

| Method | ChatGPT | Claude 2.1 | Avg. |
|--------|---------|-----------|------|
| Completion (32-Prefix) | 0.014 | 0.080 | 0.047 |
| Completion (50-Prefix) | 0.008 | 0.036 | 0.022 |
| Name Cloze | 0.311 | 0.387 | 0.349 |
| **DE-COP** | **0.720** | **0.734** | **0.727** |

**Key findings:**
- Prior black-box methods achieve only 4~35% accuracy, while DE-COP achieves **72.7% average accuracy**
- DE-COP is the **only practically viable method** for black-box copyright detection
- Prefix probing never produces correct completions for recently published books, necessitating accuracy reporting instead of AUC

### 5.4 Effect of Model Size

![Model Size Effect](/figures/decop/model_size.png)

Evaluation across LLaMA-2 7B/13B/70B shows that **larger models yield better detection performance**. This is attributed to more parameters enabling both better reasoning capabilities and higher memorization capacity.

### 5.5 Effect of Passage Length

![Passage Length Effect](/figures/decop/passage_len.png)

Evaluation across three length settings (64/128/256 tokens) on LLaMA-2 70B:
- **DE-COP outperforms all baselines on short (64) and medium (128) passages**
- **Performance drops for all methods on long (256) passages** — the full prompt reaches approximately 1024 tokens, which appears to degrade the model's reasoning ability over such large inputs

### 5.6 Effect of Logit Calibration

![Calibration Results](/figures/decop/logit_tuning_Results.png)

Calibration results on LLaMA-2 70B and ChatGPT:
- Small improvement on recently published books (Clean group)
- **Larger accuracy increase on suspect books**
- Enables more selective thresholds for distinguishing training vs. non-training data in practice

### 5.7 Effect of Paraphrasing Model

| Paraphrasing Model | Claude AUC |
|-------------------|-----------|
| Claude 2.0 | 0.948 (±0.013) |
| ChatGPT | 0.884 (±0.001) |

Claude achieves 7% higher AUC when evaluating paraphrases it generated itself, supporting the hypothesis that a model may be **slightly better at identifying its own generated paraphrases**.

### 5.8 Human Evaluation

![Human Evaluation](/figures/decop/human_eval.png)

10 human evaluators performed the same MCQA task:
- Overall average accuracy of **~34%** — barely above random chance (25%)
- **Higher performance on recently published books, lower on older books** — the **opposite pattern** from LLMs
- This strongly supports the claim that LLMs' high accuracy stems from **training data exposure**, not from paraphrase quality differences

---

## 6. Discussion and Limitations

**Selection bias:** LLMs tend to prefer certain positions in MCQA (e.g., always choosing "A"). DE-COP addresses this with 24 permutations and logit calibration, but complete elimination is difficult.

**Uncertainty in suspect group:** Since training data is undisclosed, it is uncertain whether all "suspect" books were actually used in training. Bestsellers have extensive indirect internet presence (blogs, forums, quotes) that may boost model accuracy through **indirect memorization** rather than direct training.

**Human evaluator limitations:** Some evaluators were non-native English speakers, which may have contributed to lower performance on older books written in more formal English.

---

## Key Contributions Summary

1. **DE-COP method:** Verbatim vs. paraphrase MCQA framework for detecting copyrighted content in training data, applicable to both logit-accessible and fully black-box models
2. **Two benchmarks:** BookTection (165 books) + arXivTection (50 papers)
3. **Average AUC of 0.921 on logit-accessible models** — 9.6% improvement over Min-K%-Prob
4. **72% average accuracy on fully black-box models** — dramatic improvement over prior methods (~4%)
5. **Human evaluation experiment:** Humans' low performance and opposite pattern from LLMs strengthens the evidence that DE-COP's detections are genuine

---

## Personal Comments

DE-COP's greatest strength lies in its **applicability to fully black-box models**. While most existing MIA methods require token probability access, DE-COP achieves substantial accuracy (72%) using only text outputs. This makes it a practically valuable tool for externally auditing copyright compliance of commercial models like ChatGPT and Claude.

The limitations are also clear: paraphrase quality may influence results, and popular books' extensive indirect internet presence could act as a confounder. Evaluating DE-COP on fully controlled environments like the Hubble model suite, where training data is precisely known, would yield more robust conclusions.
