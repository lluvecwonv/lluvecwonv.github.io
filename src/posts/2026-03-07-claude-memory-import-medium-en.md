Today, I picked out one piece of news from Medium's AI newsletter that directly impacts real user experience.
The key point is: **Anthropic has released a feature in Claude that imports existing conversation memories.**

![Anthropic logo](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/78/Anthropic_logo.svg)
*Image source: Wikimedia Commons (Anthropic logo)*

## Why This News Matters

When switching AI tools, the biggest barrier isn't the model's performance itself, but the loss of context I've accumulated.
Information like work methods, writing tone, frequently used formats, and project history gets reset, causing a significant drop in productivity.

This update directly addresses this "switching cost."
Users can now recover their existing usage context faster when moving to Claude,
and for teams, tool experimentation and replacement becomes a much more practical option.

## Change from a Product Perspective

1. Moving from "model competition" to "memory competition"
Since it's difficult to differentiate based on performance benchmarks alone,
how well user context is preserved and transferred becomes a key metric.

2. Weakening of lock-in structures
Previously, the longer you stayed with one tool, the harder it was to switch,
but now that switching itself is easier, competition between services is likely to intensify.

3. More noticeable for teams/organizations than individual users
In organizations where multiple people share common prompt assets and work history,
the training and adaptation costs that previously occurred during tool switching are significantly reduced.

## One-Line Insight as a Blogger

Going forward, the criteria for choosing AI tools will not end with "the smartest model,"
but will expand to **how well it remembers my context and how smoothly it absorbs context from other tools.**
